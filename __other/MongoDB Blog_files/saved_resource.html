          I can't seem to find any information on many-to-many data modeling in mongo. I don't mean many-to-few, I mean "squillions-to-squillions"

          e.g., we have millions of articles, and millions of users, there's hundreds of millions of read-events per week, and I need to model users reading documents such that I can run the following queries,

          for a user, show which articles they read
          for an article, show which users read that article
          show top articles
          show top users
          etc

          I feel like it's a pretty basic data modeling use case. I can do this data in an in-memory relational table on modest hardware.

          The only model that seems to make sense is a normalized relational mapping table, but I'm not sure that's the best approach with mongo -- especially if I have to do application level joins.

          What is the recommended approach?

                            William Zola Arthur Ketcham • 5 months ago

                            Hi Arthur -- it's easiest to explain this with an example.

                            So, let's say you have a 1->many relationship of Product -> Parts. And let's further say that each Part is a separate document, with fields 'partName', 'partNumber', and 'partQtyOnHand'.

                            So what are your choices for denormalization here? Well, you could denormalize any combination of the 3 fields from Part -> Product. How many combinations is that?

                            If you look at just 'partName', you could either denormalize it or not, so 2 combinations there. Independently, you could choose to denormalize 'partNumber' or not. If you look at them both, there's (2 * 2) combinations. In addition, you could choose to denormalize 'partQtyOnHand' or not -- if you look at all three there are (2 * 2 * 2) combinations.

                            In general, if you have M fields that are candidates for denormalization, you have 2^^M different ways to denormalize. So if there are 8 candidates for denormalization, you have 2^^8 different ways to denormalize.
